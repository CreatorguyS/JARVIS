#Slide: 2
#Header: What is SS7?
#Content: SS7 is a telecommunication protocol used for setting up and tearing down telephone calls, exchanging billing information, and more.

#Slide: 3
#Header: How Does SS7 Work?
#Content: SS7 uses signaling points to communicate messages through signaling links, allowing for efficient call setup and management.

#Slide: 4
#Header: SS7 Applications
#Content: SS7 is used for services like call forwarding, toll-free numbers, SMS, and location-based services, making it crucial for telecommunications.

#Slide: 5
#Header: SS7 Security
#Content: Despite its importance, SS7 is vulnerable to attacks like call interception and fraud. Implementing security measures is crucial to protect networks.

#Slide: 6
#Header: SS7 vs. VoIP
#Content: While SS7 is traditional, VoIP is a newer technology that offers similar services over the internet. Understanding the differences is key for modern communication.

#Slide: 7
#Header: Summary
#Content: SS7 is a vital protocol for telecommunications, enabling efficient call management. However, it requires robust security measures to prevent vulnerabilities.
