#Slide: 2
#Header: Introduction to Hacking
#Content: Hacking refers to exploiting system vulnerabilities and compromising security controls to gain unauthorized or inappropriate access to the system resources.

#Slide: 3
#Header: Types of Hacking
#Content: Hacking can be classified into various types like Black Hat, White Hat, and Grey Hat, each having different motivations and implications.

#Slide: 4
#Header: Famous Hacking Incidents
#Content: Some famous hacking incidents include the Sony Pictures hack in 2014, the Yahoo data breach in 2013-14, and the WannaCry ransomware attack in 2017.

#Slide: 5
#Header: Ethical Hacking
#Content: Ethical hacking, also known as penetration testing or white-hat hacking, involves the same tools and techniques used by malicious hackers but in a lawful manner.

#Slide: 6
#Header: Countermeasures Against Hacking
#Content: Countermeasures include setting strong passwords, using firewalls, updating software regularly, and educating users about phishing and other cyber threats.

#Slide: 7
#Header: Summary
#Content: Hacking is a serious issue. Understanding it helps us to secure our systems. Ethical hacking & countermeasures can prevent potential threats.
