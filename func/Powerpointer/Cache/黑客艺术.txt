#Slide: 2
#Header: Introduction to Hacking
#Content: Hacking refers to unauthorized intrusion into a computer or a network, often executed by skilled programmers for unauthorized data access.

#Slide: 3
#Header: Types of Hacking
#Content: Hacking can be Ethical (White Hat), Malicious (Black Hat), or Gray Hat. Each type has unique motives and implications.

#Slide: 4
#Header: Hacking Techniques
#Content: Techniques include Phishing, Password cracking, Malware, Denial of Service (DoS), etc. Each method varies in sophistication.

#Slide: 5
#Header: Consequences of Hacking
#Content: Hacking can lead to data theft, financial loss, breach of privacy, and potential legal implications.

#Slide: 6
#Header: Measures to Prevent Hacking
#Content: Measures include strong password policies, updated antivirus software, two-factor authentication, and regular system audits.

#Slide: 7
#Header: Case Studies
#Content: Examples of significant hacking incidents, their impact, and how they were addressed.

#Slide: 8
#Header: Summary
#Content: Hacking poses serious threats to privacy and security. Awareness and proactive prevention measures are key to cybersecurity.
