#Slide: 2
#Header: Definition of Hacking
#Content: Hacking refers to exploiting system vulnerabilities and compromising security controls to gain unauthorized or inappropriate access to the system resources.

#Slide: 3
#Header: Types of Hacking
#Content: Hacking types include White Hat, Black Hat, and Grey Hat hacking. Each varies based on the intent and legality of the actions.

#Slide: 4
#Header: Famous Hacks in History
#Content: Some infamous hacks include the Yahoo breach in 2013, the WannaCry ransomware attack in 2017, and the Equifax breach in 2017.

#Slide: 5
#Header: Ethical Hacking
#Content: Ethical hacking, or penetration testing, is a legal and crucial practice for maintaining system security. Ethical hackers help to identify vulnerabilities.

#Slide: 6
#Header: Preventing Hacking Attacks
#Content: Measures like strong password practices, regular system updates, and security software can help prevent hacking attacks.

#Slide: 7
#Header: The Future of Hacking
#Content: As technology evolves, so does hacking. Future challenges include quantum computing, AI-driven attacks, and increasing IoT vulnerabilities.

#Slide: 8
#Header: Summary
#Content: Hacking is a complex field with varying motives. While a threat, ethical hacking & prevention measures can help maintain security.
